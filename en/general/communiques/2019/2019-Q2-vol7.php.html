New Commissions Structure for European Stocks

On April 1, 2019, IBKR lowered its European stock commission rates for the company's tiered commission schedule. The most expensive tiers above 0.05% (5 bps) were eliminated for all 23 currencies clients are able to trade in.

For EUR, CHF, PLN, ILS and HUF-denominated products, commissions for all Monthly Trade Values up to EUR 50,000,000 are now 0.050% (5 bps). In the United Kingdom, commissions on all Monthly Trade Values up to GBP 40,000,000 are now 0.050% (5 bps).

All exchange, clearing and regulatory fees will continue to be passed through with no markup. There are no changes to the order minimums or maximums, flat rate commissions and commissions for Norway or Sweden, where commissions already start at 0.050%.

New Offering

Interactive Brokers First to Offer a Stock Yield Enhancement Program for Canadian Assets

Interactive Brokers Canada (IBC) clients participating in its Stock Yield Enhancement Program can now lend Canadian fully-paid shares of stock as part of the program. This first to the market service gives IBC clients the opportunity to lend their Canadian shares to IBC in exchange for a portion of the interest short sellers are willing to pay to borrow the shares.

IBC cash accounts with equity equivalent to US $50,000 and all IBC margin accounts are eligible to participate in the new program. Enrollment is easily completed online and program activation generally takes place overnight. After enrolling, IBC handles all program activities with no restriction on a client's ability to trade their shares. IBC clients can monitor in real-time indicative rates by configuring their Trader Workstation platform to display the Fee Rate column.

While the stock is loaned, clients maintain the ability to sell their stock anytime, and continue to recognize any profits and losses. Stocks listed on US or Canadian exchanges can be loaned.

Interactive Brokers uses its highly automated systems to identify the shares in client accounts which others are attempting to borrow. The company credits loan income to clients and provides daily activity statements detailing the quantity of shares loaned, collateral amount, market interest rate, IBC charges and net interest.

At the end of each day, IBC pays the client half of the gross amount earned by IBC from lending the shares to borrowers and retains half as compensation for coordinating the program and guaranteeing the return of the shares.

Please review the SYEP FAQ and Enrollment Instructions for additional information about participating in the program.

EXPANDED OFFERING

IBKR Bond Desk Adds Tradeweb Institutional as a Bond ATS

IBKR recently established a direct connection to Tradeweb Institutional, an alternative trading system (ATS) market that uses a Request for Quote (RFQ) model rather than a Central Limit Order Book (CLOB). On average, Tradeweb Institutional facilitates over $570 billion in notional value traded daily (TTM as of Q1 2019).

IBKR clients can send RFQs for bids and offers by opening an RFQ window in TWS, entering a bond and indicating the quantity and direction. IBKR will route the RFQ to Tradeweb, who will return a price based on the size of the RFQ.

We currently support high yield and emerging market bonds and anticipate adding other fixed income asset classes later this year.

The IBKR Client Risk Profile Tool
for Advisors

The IBKR Client Risk Profile tool is designed to help Advisors determine the most suitable investments for their clients, based on each client's risk capacity, risk need and risk tolerance. This information is collected through the distribution of a custom-designed client questionnaire. Advisors can view the scores through the Advisor Portal and use the scores to create custom pre-trade allocation groups in TWS to place orders and allocate trades for clients with similar risk profiles.

The Tool has Three Components:

Questionnaire Editor: Create a custom client questionnaire to help identify your clients' risk capacity, risk need and risk tolerance. Client responses result in a personalized "Risk Score" you can use to determine suitable investment vehicles appropriate to the client's overall risk tolerance.

Score Builder: Customize Risk Score Values for specific questions, create your own Risk Score scale, and choose the scoring calculation method for client score outcomes that complements the way you do business.

Score Viewer: View your clients' overall Risk Tolerance scores.
